EMX (Emilio) Token Overview

EMX is a blockchain-based utility token designed to power a real-world, operational application. Unlike speculative tokens, EMX is integrated into an existing ecosystem, used to access exclusive packages, participate in rewards, and fuel user growth and engagement within the app.

With a fixed max supply of 10 billion tokens, EMX features referral bonuses, a burn mechanism to reduce supply over time, and a system where a portion of claimed rewards contributes to a liquidity pool, fostering a sustainable economic model.

About EMX (Emilio Token)

EMX (Emilio) is a utility token built on the blockchain, specifically created to fuel an already operational real-world application. It serves as the primary medium for accessing exclusive packages, participating in a unique reward system, and driving user engagement within its ecosystem.

With a fixed maximum supply and controlled minting, EMX is designed for sustainable growth. Its tokenomics include a deflationary burn mechanism and contributions to a liquidity pool, ensuring value and stability for its holders and users. EMX aims to bridge the gap between blockchain technology and practical, everyday applications.

EMX Utility & Economic Flow

Discover the core functionalities and the economic design that underpins the EMX token.

Core Utility & Mechanisms
  • Core Use Case: EMX powers a real-world, operational application, used for accessing exclusive packages and participating in rewards.
  • Burn Mechanism: Each direct referral action results in 0.5% of the package payment from direct referral is designated for burn.
  • Claiming Logic & Liquidity: Upon claiming rewards, 1.5% of the claim value is sent to the `savingsLiquidityPool`, promoting ecosystem stability. 98.5% is transferred to the referrer.
  • Controlled Minting & Supply: EMX has a hard cap of 10 billion. Minting is strictly controlled by the deployer wallet, and tokens are only minted upon package purchase by whitelisted wallets.
Transparency & Future Growth
  • Smart Contract Transparency: Key on-chain functions provide transparency: `totalBurnedEMX()`, `totalClaimedBNB()`, `totalReferralRewardsGiven()`, `totalBuyersCount()`, `getPackageStats()`.
  • Planned Utility Extensions:
    • In-app upgrades and exclusive content
    • DAO governance and voting rights
    • Staking to earn additional yield
    • Cross-platform integrations with partner apps

Have Any Questions?

Find answers to commonly asked questions about EMX tokenomics and its ecosystem.

What is EMX (Emilio)?
EMX is a blockchain-based utility token created to power an existing, operational real-world application. It's used to access exclusive packages, participate in rewards, and fuel user engagement within the app.
How is EMX different from other tokens?
Unlike many speculative tokens, EMX has an immediate use case within an already operational application. Its tokenomics are designed for sustainability, including burn mechanisms and liquidity pool contributions.
What is the maximum supply of EMX?
The maximum supply of EMX is fixed at 10,000,000,000 (10 billion) tokens.
Is EMX built on its own blockchain?
EMX is a token built on an existing blockchain (e.g., Binance Smart Chain, Ethereum - specify if known). This ensures security and interoperability. Package purchases are made in BNB.
How can I acquire EMX tokens?
Users acquire EMX by purchasing one of three packages (Commander, Captain, Corporal) using BNB. These packages are tied to real services within the app.
How does the referral system work?
There's a 25% direct referral bonus on package payments (0.5% burned, 24.5% claimable) and a 0.5% unilevel bonus per level across 10 levels (handled monthly off-chain).
What is the EMX burn mechanism?
0.5% of the package payment from a direct referral is designated for burning. This reduces the total supply of EMX over time, potentially increasing its scarcity and value.
What happens when I claim my rewards?
When you claim your rewards, 1.5% of the claim value is sent to the `savingsLiquidityPool` to support the ecosystem, and the remaining 98.5% is transferred to your wallet.
What is the main use case for EMX?
EMX is primarily used to access exclusive packages and services within its operational real-world application. It also fuels the reward and referral system.
What are the planned future utilities for EMX?
Future plans include using EMX for in-app upgrades, DAO governance and voting, staking for yield, and cross-platform integrations with partner apps.
How does EMX ensure transparency?
Transparency is maintained through publicly accessible smart contract functions like `totalBurnedEMX()`, `totalClaimedBNB()`, `totalReferralRewardsGiven()`, `totalBuyersCount()`, and `getPackageStats()`.
How is the minting of EMX controlled?
EMX tokens are minted strictly upon the purchase of a package. This process is controlled by the deployer wallet, and package purchases are only available to wallets pre-approved via a whitelist.
